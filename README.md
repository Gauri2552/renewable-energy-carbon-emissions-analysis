# Energy, Carbon & Environment Analysis

A data-driven analysis of global energy production, renewable electricity trends, and carbon emissions using datasets from Our World in Data (OWID).  
This project explores how different electricity sources influence CO₂ emissions and environmental sustainability across Brazil, BRICS countries, and major global economies.

---

## Project Overview

Electricity generation is one of the largest contributors to global carbon emissions. Understanding how countries produce energy and transition towards renewable sources is essential for evaluating climate progress.

This project investigates the relationship between energy production and carbon emissions using data science techniques. The analysis focuses on Brazil while comparing patterns with BRICS countries and other major economies.

The goal is to identify trends in renewable energy adoption, fossil fuel dependence, and emission patterns through visual and statistical analysis.

---

## Objectives

- Analyse energy production trends across different countries
- Explore how electricity sources influence CO₂ emissions
- Compare renewable energy adoption across Brazil, BRICS, and global economies
- Identify long-term trends in energy and environmental sustainability
- Present findings through clear data visualisations

---

## Dataset

The analysis uses publicly available datasets from:

**Our World in Data (OWID)**  
https://ourworldindata.org

The datasets include information on:

- Electricity generation by source
- Renewable energy production
- CO₂ emissions
- Energy consumption trends
- Country-level environmental indicators

## Tools & Technologies

- **R**
- **RStudio**
- **ggplot2**
- **dplyr**
- **tidyr**
- **R Markdown**
- **Quarto RevealJS**

These tools were used to perform data cleaning, exploratory analysis, and generate reproducible presentation slides.

---

## Analysis Workflow

1. Data collection from OWID datasets
2. Data cleaning and preprocessing in R
3. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
4. Visualisation using ggplot2
5. Comparative analysis across countries
6. Generation of presentation slides using Quarto RevealJS

---

## Visualisations Included

The project includes several visualisation techniques:

- Time-series trend analysis
- Comparative line charts
- Heatmaps
- Boxplots
- Violin plots
- Decadal average bar charts

These visualisations help highlight patterns in renewable energy adoption and emission trends.

---

## Key Insights

- Brazil shows strong reliance on hydropower compared to many other countries.
- Renewable energy adoption is increasing globally, particularly in solar and wind energy.
- Some major economies still rely heavily on fossil fuels.
- Energy transitions vary significantly between developed and developing economies.
